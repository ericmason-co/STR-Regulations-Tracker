Coverage

What We Track — and What We Don’t

LawfulStay tracks government-imposed regulations on short-term rentals (STRs) — defined as rentals of residential property for fewer than 30 nights. We cover the rules that apply to hosts operating on platforms like Airbnb, Vrbo, and Booking.com.

✅ We track
  • Municipal permit & registration requirements
  • License application fees and renewal costs
  • Occupancy / transient lodging tax rates
  • Night caps and annual rental day limits
  • Primary residence and owner-occupancy rules
  • Zoning district restrictions
  • Minimum stay requirements
  • Density and quota limits
  • Platform obligations (e.g. Airbnb collect-and-remit)
  • Enforcement penalties and fines
  • Pending and proposed regulation changes
✗ We do not track
  • HOA rules and CC&R restrictions
  • Lease or rental agreement clauses
  • Building code compliance details
  • Insurance policy requirements (beyond mandates)
  • Real-time enforcement actions or citations
  • Platform-specific terms of service
  • State income tax obligations
  • Rules for commercial properties / hotels
Sources

Source Hierarchy

We apply a strict source priority order. Higher-ranked sources always override lower-ranked ones. A regulation is only published when traceable to an official or credible government-adjacent source.

  1. 1
    Official government websites & municipal codes
    City council pages, municipal code databases (e.g. Municode), planning department portals, official permit application pages. Always the primary source.
  2. 2
    Government press releases & official announcements
    Mayor's office announcements, planning department bulletins, official city communications about new or changed STR policies.
  3. 3
    Established local news outlets
    Regional newspapers and broadcast outlets reporting on city council votes, ordinance passage, or enforcement changes — used to identify leads, always verified against primary sources.
  4. 4
    Industry research & STR policy trackers
    Recognized STR policy organizations and research groups used as supplementary context — never as the sole source for a regulation claim.
Never used as sources:

Marketing pages, listing site help centers, blog posts, user-generated content, forums, social media, or any source without clear attribution to a government decision. These are categorically excluded regardless of how frequently they appear in search results.

Process

How the Update Pipeline Works

LawfulStay runs two automated research cycles per day — at 07:00 MDT and 13:00 MDT. Each cycle follows this sequence:

1
Targeted web search
The system searches for STR regulation news and legislative activity from the past 24–48 hours, focusing on jurisdictions in the day's assigned geographic region (US regions rotate by weekday; international coverage runs in parallel).
2
Source verification
Each candidate change is traced to a primary source (government website or official document). Changes that can't be sourced are discarded. The system follows the source hierarchy above and rejects any finding that relies on marketing or user-generated content.
3
Structured data extraction
Verified changes are parsed into structured fields: status, license requirements, tax rate, night cap, fees, penalties, effective date, and compliance notes. Each field is populated only when directly supported by the source.
4
Schema validation & type-checking
All records pass automated validation before being written to the database. Type errors (e.g. a boolean where a string is expected) are automatically repaired and flagged for review.
5
Static page & sitemap regeneration
All city pages, state/country hub pages, and the XML sitemap are regenerated immediately after each update cycle. Search engines are notified via IndexNow within minutes of any change.
6
Subscriber & digest notification
Users subscribed to jurisdiction alerts are notified by email when rules change. A daily digest summarizing all changes is distributed to newsletter subscribers each morning.

How We Classify Regulatory Status

Active — STRs are permitted with registration or licensing. Specific rules apply but operation is legal with compliance.

Restricted — STRs are allowed but subject to meaningful limitations: primary residence rules, hard night caps, narrow zoning, or density quotas that substantially limit most operators.

Pending — A regulation is proposed, under council review, or awaiting final vote. The current rules may differ from what's under consideration.

Banned — STRs are prohibited in most or all residential zones. Operation without an exemption is illegal and subject to penalties.

Accuracy

Accuracy Standards & Error Correction

STR regulations change frequently — sometimes with little notice. We cannot guarantee that every record is current at the exact moment you read it, but we do guarantee a documented process for maintaining accuracy:

95% of tracked jurisdictions have been verified within the last 30 days (as of July 2026). The remaining 5% are predominantly smaller international jurisdictions where official sources are updated infrequently.

Each jurisdiction page shows a last verified date. If that date is more than 60 days old, treat the data as a starting point and verify with the official source linked on the page.

How to report errors: Every jurisdiction page includes a correction form. Submissions are reviewed within 24 hours and cross-checked against the official government source before any change is made. We do not apply corrections based solely on user reports — all changes must be traceable to a primary source.

Data integrity monitoring: The database includes a small number of synthetic “canary” jurisdiction entries. These fictitious but realistic entries are used to detect unauthorized bulk copying of the database by third parties. They do not affect search results or displayed data.

Limitations

Important Limitations

LawfulStay is not legal advice.

This database is an informational research tool. It is not a substitute for advice from a licensed attorney familiar with your specific situation, property, and local jurisdiction. STR enforcement varies significantly from what written regulations say, and rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with your local government before operating.

State pre-emption is complex. In some US states, state law pre-empts local STR ordinances — meaning local rules may be unenforceable. We document local rules as written but do not adjudicate pre-emption disputes. Consult an attorney if pre-emption is relevant to your situation.

Enforcement ≠ written rules. Some jurisdictions have regulations on the books that are rarely enforced; others enforce rules more aggressively than written law suggests. We track written law only.

HOAs and leases are not covered. Even where STRs are legally permitted by government rules, HOA CC&Rs or lease clauses may prohibit them. These private agreements are entirely outside our scope.

FAQ

Common Questions About Our Data

How often is the data updated?
Twice daily — at 07:00 and 13:00 MDT. Each cycle targets a geographic region and scans for changes in the past 24–48 hours. As of July 2026, 95% of jurisdictions have been verified within the last 30 days.
What if my city isn't listed?
Use the "Request Location" button in the main database to submit a city for addition. We prioritize cities with active Airbnb markets and available government regulation data. Most requests are processed within one to two business days.
Can I use LawfulStay data commercially?
The database is available for personal research and non-commercial reference. Commercial reproduction, resale, or use as a data feed in another product requires a separate licensing agreement. Contact us to discuss licensing terms.
How do I report an error?
Use the correction form at the bottom of any jurisdiction page. Include a link to the official government source supporting your correction. All submissions are reviewed within 24 hours and cross-verified before any change is made.
Why does LawfulStay exist?
STR regulation data is scattered across thousands of government websites in dozens of languages, updated without notice, and frequently misreported by secondary sources. LawfulStay exists to provide a single, sourced, frequently verified reference that hosts, investors, property managers, and researchers can rely on — and to make that reference freely accessible rather than locked behind a paywall.
